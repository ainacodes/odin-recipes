Cinnamon Rolls

This recipe is from ambitiouskitchen

Preparation time: 2 hours

Cooking time: 20 minutes

Serving size: 9

Ingredients

Dough:

  • ¾ cup warm milk (whole milk or 2% preferred) (110 degrees F)
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons quick rise or active yeast (1/4-ounce package yeast)
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 egg plus 1 egg yolk, at room temperature
  • ¼ cup butter, melted (I prefer salted, but unsalted works, too)
  • 3 cups bread flour, plus more for dusting
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt

Filling:

  • 2/3 cup dark brown sugar (light brown sugar also works)
  • 1 ½ tablespoons ground cinnamon
  • ¼ cup butter, softened

Cream cheese frosting:

  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 3 tablespoons butter, softened
  • ¾ cup powdered sugar
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Warm milk to around 110 degrees F.
  2. Transfer warm milk to the bowl of an electric mixer and sprinkle yeast on top.
  3. Add in sugar, egg, egg yolk and melted butter. Mix until well combined.
  4. Stir in flour and salt with a wooden spoon until a dough begins to form.
  5. Place dough hook on stand mixer and knead dough on medium speed for 8 minutes. Alternatively, If you don’t want to use an electric mixer, you can use your hands to knead the dough for 8-10 minutes on a well-floured surface.
    Note: Dough should form into a nice ball and be slightly sticky. If it's TOO sticky (meaning it's sticking to the bottom of the mixer, add in 2 tablespoons more bread flour.)
  6. Transfer dough ball to a well-oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap and a warm towel. Allow dough to rise for 1 hour to 1 ½ hours, or until doubled in size.
  7. After dough has doubled in size, transfer dough to a well-floured surface and roll out into a 14x9 inch rectangle.
  8. Spread softened butter over dough, leaving a ¼ inch margin at the far side of the dough.
  9. In a small bowl, mix together brown sugar and cinnamon.
  10. Use your hands to sprinkle mixture over the buttered dough, then rub the brown sugar mixture into the butter.
  11. Tightly roll dough up, starting from the 9-inch side and place seam side down making sure to seal the edges of the dough as best you can.
    Note: You will probably need to cut off about an inch off the ends of the dough as the ends won’t be as full of cinnamon sugar as we’d want it to be.
  12. Cut into 1 inch sections with a serrated knife or floss. You should get 9 large pieces.
  13. Place cinnamon rolls in a greased 9x9 inch baking pan or round 9 inch cake pan.
  14. Cover with plastic wrap and a warm towel and let rise again for 30-45 minutes.
  15. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F.
  16. Remove plastic wrap and towel and bake cinnamon rolls for 20-25 minutes or until just slightly golden brown on the edges.
    Note: You want to underbake them a little so they stay soft in the middle, that’s why we want them just slightly golden brown.
  17. Allow them to cool for 5-10 minutes before frosting. Makes 9 cinnamon rolls.
  18. To make the frosting: In the bowl of an electric mixer, combine cream cheese, butter, powdered sugar and vanilla extract. Beat until smooth and fluffy.
  19. Spread over cinnamon rolls and serve immediately. Enjoy!

Tips

  • Don’t overheat your milk.
  • Make sure to use room temperature butter for the filling.
  • Rub the brown sugar into the butter
  • Use a room temperature egg in the dough
  • Use bread flour
  • Make sure you flour your work surface and the rolling pin
