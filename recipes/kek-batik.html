Kek Batik

This recipe is from Che Nom

Preparation time: 15 minutes

Cooking time: 10 minutes

Serving size: 9 x 9 inch

Ingredients

Kek batik:

  • 2 packs / 540-600g Marie biscuits
  • 1 piece / 200g butter (unsalted/salted)
  • ½ cup / 153g condensed milk
  • ½ cup / 50g cocoa powder
  • 1 cup / 120g Milo powder
  • ½ cup / 118ml hot water
  • ½ teaspoon salt (if using unsalted butter)
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Chocolate ganache topping:

  • 200ml / 200g whipping cream
  • 200g dark chocolate
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon butter

Instructions

Kek batik:

  1. Break the Marie biscuits into pieces.
  2. Heat the butter until melted, then add condensed milk, cocoa powder, and Milo powder. Stir until smooth and free from lumps.
  3. Add hot water, salt(if using unsalted butter), egg, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth and creamy.
  4. Pour in the broken Marie biscuits gradually.
  5. Pour the mixture into a baking pan lined with parchment paper or plastic wrap.
  6. Spread it evenly and press firmly.
  7. Chill it in the refrigerator while preparing the chocolate ganache.

Chocolate ganache topping:

  1. Heat the whipping cream in a pot, either on the stovetop or in the microwave.
  2. Once hot, pour the whipping cream over the coarsely chopped dark chocolate.
  3. Add vanilla extract and butter. Stir until fully combined, smooth, and free from lumps.
  4. Immediately pour the ganache over the surface of the kek batik.

Tips

  • Cook all the ingredients over low heat.
  • Mix the Marie biscuits gradually for easy incorporation.
  • Wait for a brief 2-3 minutes until the biscuits soften slightly before pouring them into the baking pan.
  • If possible, use high-quality chocolate for a more delicious kek batik.
